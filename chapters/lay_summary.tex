% Lay Summary - Plain language summary for general audience
Blockchain technology enables financial transactions without intermediaries through automated agreements called ``smart contracts.'' Unfortunately, these contracts can contain security flaws that attackers exploit.

A particularly dangerous flaw is the ``reentrancy attack,'' where an attacker tricks a contract into sending money multiple times before it realizes payment was already made. Since 2016, these attacks have caused hundreds of millions of dollars in losses.

This thesis makes two contributions. First, we conducted the most comprehensive study of real-world reentrancy attacks, analyzing 73 confirmed incidents over eight years and revealing increasingly sophisticated attack methods. Second, we developed ReSect, a tool that automatically detects and characterizes reentrancy attacks in milliseconds---replacing manual investigations that previously took days or weeks.

Our work helps blockchain developers protect applications, security auditors review contracts, and researchers develop new defenses.
